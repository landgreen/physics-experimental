# Simplified motion two-train challenge design

## Goal

Replace the existing two-train practice problem with a shorter version that
keeps the key step of setting two position equations equal.

## Problem

East is positive. At the same instant, one train is at the 0 m marker and
travels east at a constant 20 m/s. The other train is at the 600 m marker and
travels west at a constant 10 m/s. Students find only the meeting time and
meeting position.

## Solution

The solution states the sign convention and writes one position equation for
each train. It sets them equal at the meeting time, giving a meeting time of
20 s. It then substitutes 20 s into both equations to check the common
meeting position of 400 m.

## Constraints

- Use no unit conversions or negative position markers.
- Do not ask for the distance traveled by either train.
- Retain the existing Example card, solution details, and display-math style
  with one equal sign per line.
- Keep the card immediately before the Reading card at the end of Practice.
